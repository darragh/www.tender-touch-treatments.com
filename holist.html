HOLISTIC MASSAGE - A BRIEF HISTORY

Massage dates back thousands of years. It involves manipulation of the body tissues and has been used to promote and restore good health.

Holistic Massage also known as Swedish Massage was devised in Sweden by a man called Per Henrik Ling who was a physiotherapist and fencing master. He studied in China and used his knowledge of the body and its movements to devise his own system of massage. It is very popular worldwide.

HOW IS HOLISTIC MASSAGE PERFORMED?

You will lie down on a plinth with a special attachment for your head which allows you to breathe comfortably while lying prone (face down). Your whole body will be massaged from head to toes with grapeseed oil, which is hypo-allergenic and contains Vitamin E which moisturises the skin. A combination of strokes will be used to stretch, tone and release toxins from the muscles, while you relax in a tranquil setting. You will be left feeling relaxed and energized, ready to face the world again!

WHAT ARE THE BENEFITS?

Skin

Holistic Massage improves the skin by encouraging desquamation (the shedding of cells on the surface of the skin). By encouraging better circulation there is more efficient delivery of nutrients and oxygen to cells, and therefore more efficient waste removal.

Skeletal System

It increases joint mobility and flexibility in the shoulders, neck and arms. By reducing tension in the tissues it minimises the stress on bones and joints, reducing their need to overwork because the muscles are not performing properly.

Muscular System

Holistic Massage improves circulation and waste removal (particularly lactic acid) from muscles. This helps reduce aches and soreness and eliminates tension.

Circulatory System

Holistic Massage improves the delivery of nutrients and oxygen to the muscles and speeds up the removal of waste products and toxins. It also helps to reduce blood pressure.

Lymphatic System

Holistic Massage improves lymphatic circulation. In doing so it speeds up the removal of excess fluid and waste from the cells. It helps to reduce swelling and improves the production and delivery of white blood cells, reducing the risk of infection.

ARE THERE ANY CONTRAINDICATIONS?

TOTAL

:: Fever;
:: Contagious or infectious disease;
:: Under the influence of alcohol or drugs;

LOCAL - the massage can be performed (except on following areas)

:: Over skin diseases;
:: On undiagnosed bumps and lumps;
:: On cuts;
:: On bruises;
:: On abrasions;
:: On inflammation;
:: On scar tissue - unless at least 6 months has elapsed since the operation;
:: On sunburn;
:: Over any painful areas.

MEDICAL - the massage can only be performed with your GP's written approval

:: Medical - the massage can only be performed with your GP's written approval.
:: Cardio-vascular conditions e.g. thrombosis, phlebitis, hypertension, hypotension or any heart condition;
:: Any condition currently being treated by your GP, specialist or another complementary therapist;
:: Where medical oedema is present;
:: Osteoporosis;
:: Arthritis;
:: Nervous/psychotic disorders;
:: Recent operations;
:: Diabetes;
:: Asthma;
:: Bell's Palsy;
:: On Trapped or pinched nerves;
:: On Inflamed nerves;
:: Acute rheumatism.
